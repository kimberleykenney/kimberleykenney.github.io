# Getting started with Keep cards

Use this article to set up your Keep cards after account approval. It outlines the recommended setup sequence for new admins and links to the detailed instructions for each task.

By the end of this process, you'll have the right card type in place, cardholders assigned, cards ready for use, and spending controls set up.

---

## Before you begin

Make sure you have:

- access to your Keep account
- the names and email addresses of any team members who need cards
- a shipping address if you plan to order a physical card
- access to your phone or email if verification is required during setup

If you're setting up cards for a team, decide who needs a card, what each card will be used for, and whether spending limits should be applied before you begin.

---

## Recommended setup order

### 1. Add users who need cards

If you're issuing cards to other team members, add them to your account before creating cards. This ensures each card is assigned to the correct cardholder from the start.

For instructions, see *How to Add Team Members to Your Keep Account* and *User Roles and Permissions in Keep*.

---

### 2. Choose the right card type

Decide whether you need a virtual card, a physical card, or both.

| Card type | Best for |
|---|---|
| Virtual | Online purchases; available immediately after creation |
| Physical | In-person purchases; requires shipping and activation |
| Both | Mixed-use spending or issuing cards across a team |

For instructions, see *How to Create a Virtual Keep Card*, *How to Order a Physical Keep Card*, and *Virtual vs. Physical Cards: Which Option to Use*.

---

### 3. Assign the card to the right cardholder

When you create a card, assign it to the person who will use it. This helps you track spending accurately, control access, and maintain accountability across your team.

For instructions, see *How to Assign a Keep Card to a User* and *How Card Ownership Works in Keep*.

---

### 4. Activate the card if required

Activate the card before using it for purchases. Depending on the card type, activation may be automatic or may require a separate step before the card can be used.

For instructions, see *How to Activate Your Keep Card* and *Card Activation: Virtual vs. Physical Cards*.

---

### 5. Review card details and limits

Before spending begins, review the card details available in Keep. This helps you confirm the card is ready to use, understand the available credit, and check any spending limits or controls that apply.

For instructions, see *How to View Your Keep Card Details*, *How to Check Available Credit*, and *How to Set Spending Limits on Keep Cards*.

---

### 6. Add the card to a digital wallet

Add the card to Apple Wallet or Google Wallet if the cardholder plans to use it for contactless or mobile payments. This is the fastest way to make the card available for tap-to-pay and mobile purchases.

For instructions, see *How to Add Your Keep Card to a Digital Wallet* and *Supported Wallets and Mobile Payment Options*.

---

### 7. Start using and monitoring the card

Once setup is complete, the cardholder can begin making purchases and you can begin monitoring spending in Keep.

For instructions, see *How to Track Card Transactions*, *How to Monitor Team Spending*, and *How to Freeze or Replace a Keep Card*.

---

## After setup, you can

- make purchases online or in person
- track transactions in your dashboard
- monitor spending across your team
- update card access, limits, or controls as needed
