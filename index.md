## Context

This piece shows how I would audit and revise a help center article for first-time digital wallet setup using a Keep Card. The goal was to make the content clearer, more task-focused, and easier to scan.

## Audit Summary

The original article had a few common usability issues:

- no clear prerequisites before setup
- steps were not grouped in a logical order
- Apple Wallet and Google Wallet instructions were mixed together
- important actions were buried in long explanatory text
- the article was difficult to scan quickly

## Before

The original version opened with too much explanation before helping the user take action. Key setup instructions were embedded in paragraphs, making it harder to follow the task from start to finish.

## Revised Help Article

### Add Your Keep Card to a Digital Wallet

You can add your Keep Card to a supported digital wallet to make secure, contactless payments from your mobile device.

#### Before you begin

Make sure that:

- your Keep account is active
- your Keep Card has been issued
- you have access to your mobile device
- your device supports Apple Wallet or Google Wallet

#### Add your card to Apple Wallet

1. Open the **Wallet** app on your iPhone.
2. Tap the **+** icon in the top-right corner.
3. Select **Debit or Credit Card**.
4. Tap **Add a Different Card**.
5. Enter your Keep Card details manually or scan your card.
6. Follow the prompts to verify your card.

#### Add your card to Google Wallet

1. Open the **Google Wallet** app.
2. Tap **Add to Wallet**.
3. Select **Payment card**.
4. Enter your Keep Card details manually or scan your card.
5. Follow the prompts to verify your card.

#### Verify your card

Depending on your account settings, you may be asked to verify your card using:

- a one-time code sent to your email or phone
- your Keep dashboard

#### Troubleshooting

**Card not accepted**

- Confirm that your card is active in your Keep account.
- Make sure your device supports digital wallet payments.

**Verification issues**

- Check that your contact information is up to date.
- Restart the app or device and try again.

## Why this revision is stronger

This version uses a task-first structure, separates Apple Wallet and Google Wallet instructions clearly, and gives users the information they need before they begin. The result is a shorter, more scannable article that better supports successful first-time setup.
